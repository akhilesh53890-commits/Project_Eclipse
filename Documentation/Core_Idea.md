# Project Eclipse - Core Idea & Design Overview

## 1. Core Player Objective
**What is the player trying to do?**
- **Primary Actions:** **Escape & Solve** (supported by **Survive & Explore**).
- **Goal:** Escape the sealed Blackwood Astronomical Observatory complex while solving light-prism and celestial alignment puzzles and evading cosmic shadow manifestations during a total solar eclipse.

---

## 2. Player Motivation
**Why? What motivates the player?**
- **Motivation:** **Uncovering the Truth & Preventing Permanent Madness.**
- Dr. Gabriel Vance initially arrives to investigate a total communications blackout. Upon entering, he discovers his own repressed memories and past research are deeply tied to the facility's secret experiments. He must gather lost logs, reconstruct the final hours of the observatory crew, and reverse the eclipse ritual before his sanity degrades completely, trapping him in the umbral realm forever.

---

## 3. The Villain / Primary Antagonist
**Who is the villain?**
- **Antagonist:** **"UMBRA" (The Eclipse Manifestation)**
- Born from a cosmic signal intercepted by Chief Astronomer Dr. Elena Rostova, **UMBRA** is an ethereal, light-consuming entity that stalks the player throughout the facility. It feeds on darkness and player panic, manifesting physical geometry shifts and auditory hallucinations as the eclipse reaches totality.

---

## 4. Key Differentiators & Unique Selling Points (USPs)
**What makes this game different?**
1. **Dynamic Sanity Geometry:** The facility's corridors, doors, and rooms alter their physical structure in real-time based on the player's Sanity Meter.
2. **Interactive Light-and-Shadow Mechanics:** Light serves as a shelter, puzzle tool, and defense. Focusing the flashlight beam stuns shadow entities and activates light-prisms, but rapidly drains battery life.
3. **Vintage Analog & Celestial Puzzles:** Puzzle solving involves manipulating 1990s analog optics, star charts, and telescope focal lenses to align light beams.
4. **Atmospheric Dread over Cheap Jump Scares:** Focuses on psychological tension, spatial disorientation, and rich environmental storytelling.
